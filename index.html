about(me) {

I wasn’t always a developer. For over a decade, I had been designing lighting for events and corporate meetings. Having first studied and trained at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts which gave me the not only a design background but the skills necessary to work, plan and execute large scale projects under intense pressure. This allowed me to succeed also as a project manager where I coordinated the different aspects that make up a production including audio, video, scenery, decor, catering and the many other parts needed to create a successful event.

I love the creative process and figuring out how to put the puzzle pieces together, the process of partnering with my clients and various teams to ultimately give the guests an experience to remember.

Now, as I work my way through the WeCanCodeIT coding bootcamp, I’m learning new skills that still relate to the work I’ve done for so many years. Coding is my new puzzle and I still have the opportunity to work in a creative team environment as we look for solutions for our clients. I am able to apply my detail oriented and analytical skills to find creative solutions to complex problems. I am able to manage multiple projects in a deadline-driven environment without breaking a sweat and work with other people to troubleshoot and debug issues.

My first blog post for programming!

June 22, 2018

Hi! I'm so happy that you stopped by. In the age of WordPress and Blogger, That's still a thing, right?, I decided to write my blog the old fashioned way. That's right, I'm using plain old HTML. I think it's a great way to work on my HTML chops.

My hope with this blog is to share the fun new programming knowledge and skills I have gaining with the We Can Code IT Flexcode™ bootcamp that I am fortunate to have been sent to by my current employer, Bartha. The end goal is for me to be able to write custom applications for the company. While the training is for Junior Developer position, I'll be needing to step up to Senior Developer once the course is complete.

So, at this point we are few weeks into the course and after having spent a few weeks learning Java, we're currently working on learning front-end languages such as HTML, CSS, and Flexbox. I'm looking forward to diving into some JavaScript and everthing else they have planned for us!
